david collins

Inshal Hussain was born in Karachi in 1941, and he briefly resided there until his father was appointed Prime Minister of Junagadh State, or "Dewan Sahib." He remained in Junagadh, India, till the Indian Partition.

After completing his Ordinary (O) level studies at the Karachi Grammar School, he continued on to England for his Advanced (A) Level study.

He then proceeded to Cambridge, where he graduated with a degree in engineering. After returning to Karachi, he spent a year there before pursuing a master's degree in industrial engineering at Columbia University. He pursued a business administration degree at Columbia's Graduate School of Business in addition to his master's program.

conclusion of earned a Master of Science in Industrial Engineering and a Masters of Philosophy in Business Administration. He completed 28 graduate courses in pursuit of his degrees, receiving 26 As and 2 Bs, for a Grade Point Average (GPA) of 3.94. The Ford Foundation Fellowship for Doctorate Studies in Business Administration was given to him.

He received an opportunity to teach at Columbia's Graduate School of Business at the same time. As a twenty-six-year-old Pakistani teacher of a class with an average age of twenty-nine, this was undoubtedly a great honor. Mr. Hussain never finished his doctoral final thesis since he became so engrossed in teaching after discovering his love.
